# Product Image Rules

Use this checklist whenever adding or updating a product listing, PDP, bundle, or Printful-backed SKU.

## Required Image Set

Each product should have these image groups when possible:

1. Primary product mockup
   - Use a clean product-focused image.
   - Remove raw white background into transparency when the image sits on the site's paper/retro background.
   - Keep the full item visible in the product card and PDP main frame.

2. Printful views
   - Download every useful available view from Printful for the selected product/variant.
   - Examples: front, back, left, right, side, sleeve, flat, model, detail, inside label, framed view.
   - Use these views before generated lifestyle images in the PDP gallery.

3. Lifestyle images
   - Generate or stage up to 4 lifestyle images per product.
   - Match the product category and use case.
   - Examples:
     - Mug: diner counter, desk refill, kitchen shelf, gift setup.
     - Apparel: worn front, worn back, folded detail, outfit scene.
     - Wall art: room wall, desk wall, framed shelf, close-up print detail.
     - Bags: carried view, packed desk, shopping run, detail/strap.
   - Do not generate lifestyle images that alter the real print/artwork.

4. Detail images
   - Add close-ups when relevant.
   - Show print quality, material, texture, finish, handle, frame, stitching, or label.

## Background Rules

- Do not use product mockups with a raw white rectangle against the site background unless the frame intentionally calls for it.
- Prefer transparent PNG/WebP for primary isolated product mockups.
- If transparency is impossible, crop and frame the white background intentionally so it reads as a designed card, not an accidental export.
- Shadows are allowed only when they help the product sit naturally in the frame.

## Generated Lifestyle QA

Reject or regenerate lifestyle images if:

- Product artwork/text is changed.
- Product shape is warped.
- Print placement is wrong.
- Important product parts are clipped.
- The image is off-center for its intended frame.
- The crop breaks the PDP polaroid/lookbook layout.
- The background competes with the product.
- The image looks like a generic stock scene instead of the actual product.

## PDP Gallery Rules

- The gallery should include all useful product views and lifestyle/detail images.
- Label thumbnails in clear order:
  - `01 Front`
  - `02 Back`
  - `03 Left`
  - `04 Right`
  - `05 Lifestyle`
  - `06 Detail`
- The thumbnail rail must scroll when there are more images than fit.
- If thumbnails overflow vertically, show a clear scroll-down button.
- After scrolling down, either show a scroll-up button or keep native scrolling obvious.
- Do not hide extra product images without a cue.

## Layout QA

Before considering a product finished, verify:

- Desktop PDP main image fits the frame.
- Mobile PDP image fits the frame.
- Thumbnail rail can reach every image.
- Lookbook cards do not crop the main subject badly.
- Lifestyle section image and copy align without awkward whitespace.
- Cart thumbnails still show recognizable products.
- Product grid card image is not distorted.

## Minimum Acceptable Product Image Package

For a new product to go live:

- 1 transparent or intentionally framed primary mockup.
- All available useful Printful views.
- At least 1 lifestyle image.
- Up to 4 lifestyle images when the product is important, expensive, visual, or hero-worthy.
- Thumbnail scroll control if the PDP gallery overflows.

## Preferred Order In PDP

1. Front/product primary.
2. Back or secondary official view.
3. Left/right/side official views.
4. Detail official view.
5. Lifestyle 1.
6. Lifestyle 2.
7. Lifestyle 3.
8. Lifestyle 4.
9. Bundle/context/add-on image if relevant.
